Swearing-In Address  |  Second Term

Speech Delivered by the Honourable Speaker
to the Council of Buwambwa Sub-County

Honourable Mutonyi Janet — Sub-County Speaker
✦
Opening & Salutations

The Resident District Commissioner of this great district, in whose presence we gather today — we salute you.

The Chief Administrative Officer, the Sub-County Chief, the distinguished heads of departments, invited guests, members of the press, and all protocol observed — we acknowledge and honour you all.

And most especially — the men and women who make this council what it is — the honourable councilors of Buwambwa Sub-County, both those who have walked this journey with me since the very beginning and those who join us fresh with new energy and new purpose — I welcome you all with a heart full of gratitude.

To my pioneer councilors — those who sat with me in the very first session of this council when there was no script to follow, no tradition to lean on, and no predecessor to guide us — I salute you by name. [Honourable ________________], [Honourable ________________], [Honourable ________________] and all who were there from the first day: you are the foundation of everything we have built. History has already written your names. This council shall never forget you.

Opening Prayer of Purpose

Before I say another word, I want to acknowledge that I stand here not by my own strength alone. I stand here because of the grace of God, the confidence of this council, and the trust of the people of Buwambwa Sub-County. To them, I owe everything. And it is to them that every decision I make from this chair shall be dedicated.

A Historic Moment

Honourable councilors, today is not an ordinary day in the life of Buwambwa Sub-County.

When this sub-county was established, someone had to be first. Someone had to sit in this chair without a template, without a manual, and without a predecessor to call for advice in the middle of the night. Five years ago, this council placed that burden — and that honour — upon my shoulders. I was humbled then. I remain humbled now.

But today marks something even more significant. Today, after a free and competitive election of the council, you have chosen to send me back. You did not have to. There were other candidates. There were other choices. And yet, when the votes were counted — twelve votes in my favour, with the remaining five shared among the field — this council spoke with clarity and conviction.

"You did not just re-elect a Speaker. You made a statement — that Buwambwa Sub-County believes in continuity with purpose, in experience paired with vision, and in leadership that is accountable to the people it serves."

I do not take this lightly. I will not waste it.

Reflecting on the First Term

Honourable councilors, allow me to briefly look back before we turn our faces firmly forward. Because a leader who does not reflect does not grow, and a council that does not account for the past cannot be trusted with the future.

Our first term was not perfect. I will not stand here and pretend otherwise. No first term ever is — and ours had the added weight of being the very first term in the history of this sub-county's council. We were building the house while living in it.

But I am proud to say: the first term was not bad. Far from it.

We introduced and upheld the dignity of council proceedings. We established the culture of the gown — the official regalia that reminds every person in this chamber that when they sit here, they are not sitting for themselves. They are sitting for the people. That gown is a symbol of service, of seriousness, of sacrifice. And under my tenure, it became a standard, not a suggestion.

We worked to improve the roads of our sub-county — the paths that connect our communities, that allow our mothers to reach the health centre, our children to reach the school, and our farmers to reach the market.

We paid attention to the health of our people. We advocated, we followed up, and we ensured that the voice of Buwambwa was heard at every level where health decisions were being made.

We championed programmes for our youth and for our women — because a sub-county that does not invest in its women and its young people is a sub-county that is investing in its own decline. We refused to do that.

We worked on revenue collection and on ensuring that the resources of this sub-county were used transparently and accountably. Not perfectly — but honestly and with intention.

And we maintained security. We cooperated with all relevant agencies to ensure that the people of Buwambwa could sleep at night without fear.

These are not small things. These are the threads of a fabric that we began weaving five years ago. And that fabric is not yet complete.

Acknowledging the Challenges

But honesty demands that I speak not only of our successes.

There were moments in our first term when the dignity of this council was tested. There were instances of disrespect — toward the institution, toward the chair, toward the procedures that make a council function. I will not dwell on the past. I will not call names. What I will say is this:

Those days are behind us. This new term begins with a new covenant of respect — respect for one another, respect for the people who sent us here, and respect for this council as an institution. We may disagree — and we will, because that is the nature of democracy. But we shall disagree with dignity. We shall debate with honour. And we shall always remember that the moment we disrespect this house, we disrespect Buwambwa.

I will hold myself to that standard first. And I ask every councillor in this room to hold themselves to it too.

The Central Mission of This Term: Our Office

Honourable councilors, let me now speak about what I consider the central physical mission of this second term.

For five years, this sub-county council has operated without a permanent home. We have conducted our business, we have made our decisions, we have served our people — but we have done so without a building that says to the world: here sits the council of Buwambwa Sub-County.

That changes under this term.

I am pleased to report that we have already taken the foundational steps. The land has been acquired. The bricks have been purchased. The groundwork has been laid — literally and figuratively. What now remains is the will, the unity, and the sustained effort to turn those bricks into walls, those walls into rooms, and those rooms into a council chamber worthy of the people of Buwambwa.

"We have the land. We have the bricks. What we must now supply is something that no procurement can provide — our collective commitment to see this through. By the time this term ends, Buwambwa Sub-County Council will have a home."

This is not just a construction project. This is a statement of permanence. It tells every future council, every future speaker, every future generation of Buwambwa, that those who came before them took their work seriously enough to build something that would outlast them.

I am calling on every councillor in this room to become an ambassador for this project. Talk about it in your communities. Celebrate every milestone. And when it is done, know that your name is written into the story of this building.

My Call to This Council: Unity

Honourable councilors, I have sat in this chair. I know what it takes. And I know that no speaker — no matter how experienced, no matter how dedicated — can do anything of lasting value without a united council.

Unity does not mean uniformity. You are different people, from different villages, representing different communities, with different opinions. That is exactly as it should be. But when we enter this chamber, we must be united in one thing above all others: our commitment to the people of Buwambwa Sub-County.

I will not lead a divided council. I will not allow factional politics to reduce this body to a battleground of personal interests. Every time we fight each other, the people of Buwambwa lose. And they cannot afford to keep losing.

I am therefore making a personal commitment to every councillor in this room: my door is open. My ear is available. My respect for you is unconditional — because the people gave you this seat, and the people's choice deserves my respect. Come to me with your concerns before they become conflicts. Let us solve our problems inside these walls so that we can face our communities with a unified front.

My Call to This Council: Accountability

The people of Buwambwa Sub-County did not send us here to sit and warm these chairs. They sent us here to be accountable stewards of every resource, every decision, and every vote that passes through this council.

Accountability means attending sessions. Accountability means participating in debate. Accountability means going back to your communities and reporting — not just when there is good news, but especially when there are challenges. Accountability means that this council never becomes an exclusive club that speaks only to itself.

I will set the standard from this chair. Every session will be conducted with order, transparency, and fairness. No voice will be silenced without cause. No agenda will be hidden. And no decision will be made that cannot be explained to the smallest child in the most remote village of Buwambwa.

My Call to This Council: Hard Work

Finally — there is no substitute for hard work. None.

The office we are building will not construct itself. The roads our people need will not be advocated for unless we advocate loudly and persistently. The health services, the youth programmes, the women's initiatives, the revenue — none of it will happen if we treat this as a ceremonial position.

I have never treated this role as ceremonial. I do not intend to start now.

I am asking every councillor here to bring their full self to this work. Not part of yourself — all of yourself. The people of Buwambwa are counting on us. Let us not be the generation that let them down.

A Vision for Buwambwa

I want to share with you the vision that will guide my speakership in this second term. It is simple enough to remember and strong enough to sustain us through difficulty:

"Buwambwa Rising: United in Service, Built on Integrity, Committed to Progress."

United in Service — because we serve together or we fail apart.

Built on Integrity — because without trust, nothing we build will stand.

Committed to Progress — because every session of this council must leave Buwambwa better than it found it.

These are not just words. They will be the lens through which I evaluate every decision, every debate, and every action of this council. I ask you to hold me to them. I ask you to hold yourselves to them.

To the New Councilors

To those of you joining this council for the first time — welcome. Welcome to one of the most important responsibilities your community has ever placed on your shoulders.

You will learn quickly that this work is not glamorous. It is long hours, hard conversations, difficult decisions, and occasionally thankless service. But it is also the most meaningful work you will ever do. Because when you get it right, real people live better lives. Children go to better schools. Mothers receive better care. Roads improve. Businesses grow. Communities thrive.

That is why we are here. Never forget it. And when the days are hard — and they will be hard — remember the faces of the people who voted for you. Let those faces be your fuel.

Closing: A Covenant with Buwambwa

Honourable councilors, I want to close with a commitment — not a promise, but a covenant. Because promises can be forgotten. A covenant is a binding declaration before God and before the people.

I covenant to conduct this council with dignity, fairness, and impartiality. I will not favour one side over another. I will not silence legitimate debate. I will not use this chair for personal gain or political advantage.

I covenant to pursue the construction of our sub-county council office with every tool available to me. By the end of this term, we will have built something that our grandchildren will point to with pride.

I covenant to uphold unity, demand accountability, and model hard work — in every session, in every meeting, in every interaction that bears the name of this council.

And I covenant to remain a servant. Not a ruler. Not a figure. A servant — of this council, of this sub-county, and of the God who placed me here for such a time as this.

Buwambwa Sub-County, your Speaker is ready. Your council is assembled. Your future is waiting.

Let us rise to meet it.

God bless Buwambwa Sub-County. God bless the Republic of Uganda. And God bless every honourable member seated in this chamber today.

I thank you.

Honourable Mutonyi Janet
